About Me

I am currently a research engineer at the Computation and Cognition Lab at Stanford. I graduated from the University of Pennsylvania where I studied computer science (BSE) and physics, and am currently located in San Francisco, CA. I am interested in probabilistic modeling and computational cognitive science, and its applications in solving problems in games, computer vision, and speech.

In my free time, I enjoy jamming with friends and having long discussions about things I read. I am also interested in sleight of hand, and I do a bit of closeup card magic.

I think a lot — from science to philosophy, politics to relationships — and I write about some of it in my blog. No valid evidence that reading it will change your life.

The final frontier is not the universe around us. It is the one inside our minds.
